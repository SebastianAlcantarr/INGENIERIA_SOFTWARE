Core Concepts

Definition

The Limit Definition of a Derivative

The derivative of a function f(x) at a point 'a' is the limit of the difference quotient as h approaches zero. It represents the instantaneous rate of change.

f'(x) = lim(h→0) [f(x+h) - f(x)] / h

Interpretation

Slope and Rate of Change

Geometrically, the derivative is the slope of the tangent line to the function's graph at a given point. Physically, it represents the instantaneous rate of change of a quantity.

Differentiation Rules

Power Rule

If f(x) = xⁿ, then f'(x) = nxⁿ⁻¹

Product Rule

If h(x) = f(x)g(x), then h'(x) = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x)
